The VST ATLAS Survey is a panoramic optical survey, which will complement the proposed VST and VISTA Hemisphere Surveys in the South. The initial aim of ATLAS is to survey 4500 deg2 of the Southern Sky at high galactic latitudes to comparable depths to the SDSS in the North. The VST ATLAS will be the first step towards a panoramic digital survey of the Southern Sky in the optical bands. Science drivers and uses are:

  • to determine the dark energy equation of state by detecting 'baryon wiggles' in the power-spectrum of ∼450000 z∼0.7 Luminous Red Galaxies (LRGs), using either photo-z from VST ATLAS + VHS imaging or spectroscopic redshifts via the new AAOmega instrument;
  • to probe for dark energy including LRG z-space distortion, the Integrated Sachs-Wolfe Effect and the dependence of QSO lensing on redshift;
  • to colour select QSO candidates out to z∼7.

In addition, the VST ATLAS+AAOmega will feed VLT with rare galaxy and QSO targets for high resolution spectroscopy, constituting a new and uniquely powerful tool for survey cosmology.
For more information see the ATLAS home page.
